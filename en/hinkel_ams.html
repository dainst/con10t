The AMS-System

Figure 1: Kartenblätter NF-34 to NA-36 The alphanumeric codification of archaeological sites used in Hinkel's publication series “The Archaeological Map of the Sudan“ is largely based on the International Map of the World, founded in 1913. The International Map of the World consists of maps in the scale of 1:1.000.000, each of which holding an alphanumeric designation based on their location. Map sheets depicting areas in the northern hemisphere carry the identification N as well as a designation referring to their respective geographic latitude (a letter) and longitude (a two-digit number). The Sudan (including the Republic of South Sudan) is depicted in the map sheets ranging from NF-34 to NA-36 (see Fig. 1). In addition to these standardized designations used since the 1960s, the archive of Friedrich W. Hinkel also contains material referring to an older system in which the alphanumeric code was replaced by a sequence of numbers with up to three digits (see Fig. 1).

Figure 2: Kartenblatt NE-37-A Every 1:1.000.000 map sheet of the International Map of the World is divided into 16 maps in the scale of 1:250.000, named A to P. This classification of the International Map of the World has been used for the registration of sites by the Sudan Antiquities Service since the early 20th century and was later refined by Friedrich Wilhelm Hinkel by adding three additional layers of geographic classification. The map sheets with a scale of 1:250.000 were thus divided into 24 rectangles representing a longitude of 15' numbered 1 to 24 (i.e. Fig. 2). Each of these rectangles were then divided into 25 units named A to Y representing a longitude of 3'.

Due to this refinement of the system and the resulting AMS-numbers it is possible to locate archaeological sites in the Sudan within an area of ca. 5 by 5 km using only the alphanumeric designation as a reference. The system also allows for the registration for less securely located sites and findings based on a more general description of their original location.
