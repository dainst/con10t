The site

The archaeological site of Ayamonte lies more than 3,000 nautical miles west of the Phoenician motherland and is thus the westernmost known Phoenician colony of the early expansion wave of the 8th century BC. The estuary of the Guadiana – the river today forms the border between Spain and Portugal – in the Atlantic offered sheltered landing places and ideal conditions for the development of a settlement and for interaction with indigenous trading partners from the resource-rich hinterland.

The project

Remains of the Phoenician cremation necropolis first came to light during construction work in 2007 and were documented in rescue excavations by Elisabet García Teyssandier and Benjamín Cabaco Encinas. The Madrid Department of the German Archaeological Institute took part in the evaluation of the excavation results at the necropolis at the invitation of Juan Aurelio Pérez Macías (University of Huelva / corresponding member of the DAI). Building on that work, a research excavation was carried out in the Phoenician necropolis in September 2013 under the direction of Dirce Marzoli (DAI Madrid) in cooperation with E. García Teyssandier; the excavation was authorized by the Andalusian heritage authority (Delegación General del Patrimonio Arqueológico y de Instituciones Museisticas de la Junta de Andalucía, Sevilla / Delegación de Cultura de Huelva).

The project is administratively based at the DAI in Cluster 6 “Connecting Cultures”. The financing has come from the DAI research budget. The project brings together the results from the archaeological excavations and from a number of other disciplines (incl. anthropology, archaeobotany, archaeometry, geography). The finds are kept at the Museo de Huelva.

The database

There was detailed digital documentation of the excavations of 2013: alongside the comprehensive documentation of features using 3D recording by Arie Kai-Browne there was in particular a relational FileMaker database for field research projects developed by the DAI’s IT unit (iDAI.field 1), which was maintained by Ulrich Stockinger. The data were migrated for publication into the successor application iDAI.field 2, an open-source solution that is based on Electron, Angular, Typescript and PouchDB and utilizes a number of web technologies (JavaScript, HTML, CSS).

Instructions for use

When you open the database, you find yourself first of all in the tab “Overview”, as indicated by the home icon. This is where the hierarchically arranged places and trenches of the project are managed; via these you can access the finds and features. In iDAI.field 2, the various archaeological data (e.g. trenches, features, finds) are listed under the umbrella term “resources“. With all resources, a click on the info symbol opens up the associated data. The documentation is available in German and Spanish, separated by “ / “.The database interface can be set to either German or English (File>Settings>Language or File>Settings>Language). The right-angled arrow pointing down and to the right leads to the subordinate resources, while the arrow pointing up and to the right opens up the resource(s) within an excavation trench in a new tab (“Switch to operation” or “Show in context of an operation”). You can open several trenches alongside each other and switch between them.

You can also switch between map view and list view by using the icons at the top right. The following instructions relate to map view. Here the selected entry is displayed dynamically on the left against a grey background or indicated on the map where geodata (polygons, polylines, points) are available. The current path within the project or trench is shown top left; by clicking on the path you will be able to navigate quickly to another level of the hierarchy. By clicking the magnifier button you can activate (or deactivate) the “extended search mode”. This enables you to search through large amounts of data; that is, all resources of the project are included in the “Overview” tab, as all resources of the respective trench are in the “operation” tabs. Otherwise only resources from the immediately subordinate hierarchy level are shown.

Using the input field, you can carry out free text searches within the resources that are currently being displayed. At the present time search terms are checked against the fields “Identifier” and “Short description” of the resources. The search performed is a so-called prefix search, i.e. the beginning of a term (after a space or a hyphen) is searched for; upper case and lower case are ignored in searches. This means the search term “fragment”, for instance, returns “Amphore, fragmentarisch erhalten” and “Gefäßkeramik-Fragmente”, but not the entries “Frgt.”, “Keramikfragmente” or “Wandfragment”. Unknown characters can be substituted by various permitted characters within square brackets for wildcard searches. For example, “AYA13-10[14]3” will return the results “AYA13-1013” and “AYA13-1043”. Wildcard searches with * are not supported.

Extended search mode allows you to add search criteria using the plus button. For instance, you can restrict results to resources with images. The funnel icon to the right of this allows you to limit the search to one category (e.g. “burial” or “mollusc”). Search criteria and category filters can be combined and also used without search text. The search results are displayed on the left in the list as well as on the map. If the search filter used produces no results in the current context, search results from other contexts are shown under the text field. You can access them by mouse click.

The "Tools" menu allows you to manage images. Here you can search and select all images from a project. Next to the input field for searches you will find a page navigation to browse backwards and forwards as well as a slider to adjust the size of the image preview.
