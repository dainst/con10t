The data published here are part of the documentation of the excavation in the Phoenician necropolis of Ayamonte (Huelva, Andalusia, Spain) conducted in September 2013. They are a digital supplement to the published monographs: D. Marzoli - E. García Teyssandier, Die phönizische Nekropole von Ayamonte (Huelva, Andalusien, Spanien). Bericht zur Ausgrabung im Jahre 2013, ihren Vor- und Begleituntersuchungen, MB 37 (Wiesbaden 2018); D. Marzoli - E. García Teyssandier, La necrópolis fenicia de Ayamonte (Huelva). Memoria de la excavación del año 2013, estudios previos y complementarios, Arqueología Monografías (Sevilla 2019).

The site

The archaeological site of Ayamonte lies more than 3,000 nautical miles west of the Phoenician motherland and is the westernmost known Phoenician colony of the early expansion wave of the 8th century BC. The estuary of the Guadiana – the river today forms the border between Spain and Portugal – in the Atlantic offered sheltered landing places and ideal conditions for the development of a settlement and for interaction with indigenous trading partners from the resource-rich hinterland.

The project

Remains of the Phoenician cremation necropolis first came to light during construction work in 2007 and were documented in rescue excavations by Elisabet García Teyssandier and Benjamín Cabaco Encinas. The Madrid Department of the German Archaeological Institute took part in the evaluation of the excavation results at the necropolis at the invitation of Juan Aurelio Pérez Macías (University of Huelva / corresponding member of the DAI). Building on that work, a research excavation was carried out in the Phoenician necropolis in September 2013 under the direction of Dirce Marzoli (DAI Madrid) in cooperation with E. García Teyssandier; the excavation was authorized by the Andalusian heritage authority (Delegación General del Patrimonio Arqueológico y de Instituciones Museisticas de la Junta de Andalucía, Sevilla / Delegación de Cultura de Huelva).

The project is administratively based at the DAI in Cluster 6 “Connecting Cultures”. The financing has come from the DAI research budget. The project brings together the results from the archaeological excavations and from a number of other disciplines (incl. anthropology, archaeobotany, archaeometry, geography). The finds are kept at the Museo de Huelva.

The database

There was detailed digital documentation of the excavations of 2013: alongside the comprehensive documentation of features using 3D recording by Arie Kai-Browne there was in particular a relational FileMaker database for field research projects developed by the DAI’s IT unit (iDAI.field 1), which was maintained by Ulrich Stockinger. The data were migrated for publication into the successor application iDAI.field 2, an open-source solution that is based on Electron, Angular, Typescript and PouchDB and utilizes a number of web technologies (JavaScript, HTML, CSS).

Instructions for use

You can access the online database’s landing page via this link. You will find an option to search the data on the left, you can switch to the hierarchical view or display a specific category of data only (e.g. graves or pottery). Using the funnel symbol below the search window, you can restrict your search result to a category or remove the selected filter. You can also use the category filter without search text, e.g. to display all burials or molluscs. In iDAI.field 2, the diverse archaeological data are referred to by the collective term "resources".

For all resources, a click opens the detailed view: At the top, the hierarchically superordinate resources are displayed, below you will find the information on the resource selected. You can return to the search result (link below the search window), open one of the superordinate resources or start a new search. By clicking on the chain link symbol, you can display a permalink to the resource displayed. In the overview of the resources, a click on the left will take you to the higher-level resources, a click on the right to the lower-level resources if there is a corresponding arrow symbol. The map view automatically shows you the resource if geodata (polygons, multipoints, points) are available for it. Click on an image to open the large view and the detailed information about it.

The documentation is available in German and Spanish, in free text fields both versions are shown separated by " / ". The language selection is made automatically based on the settings of your internet browser - this also applies to the general interface, which is currently either German or English. The house symbol next to the search window will take you back to the home page.

Your search terms are currently matched with the fields "Identifier" and "Short description" of the resources. This is a so-called prefix search, i.e. it always checks for the beginning of a term (after spaces or hyphens); upper or lower case is ignored in the search. This means that the search term “fragment”, for instance, returns “Amphore, fragmentarisch erhalten” and “Gefäßkeramik-Fragmente”, but not the entries “Frgt.”, “Keramikfragmente” or “Wandfragment”. Unknown characters can be substituted by various permitted characters within square brackets for wildcard searches. For example, “AYA13-10[14]3” will return the results “AYA13-1013” and “AYA13-1043”. Wildcard searches with * are not supported.
