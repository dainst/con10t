The Photographs and Slides

The photographs and slides, dating back to the early 1940s, represent the largest number of all digitized items in the Archive of Friedrich W. Hinkel. The archive contains about 45.895 photographs and slides (33.744 photographs belonging to 1.652 negative films as well as 12.151 slides), which were scanned in the course of the digitization project. Duplicates and reproductions of other (already scanned) material were excluded from digitization. Due to the large period of time covered by the photographic material its does not only include pictures made in the Sudan but also photographic documentation of Dr. Hinkel's travels as an employee of the Deutschen Bauakademie (since 1971 called Bauakademie der DDR) as well as private journeys within Germany and all over the world. The digitized photographs and slides were structured according to the AMS-system. Pictures made outside of the Sudan were also associated with their respective places in the iDAI.gazetteer, through which one can access the data based on their location.

In order to retain the quotation system introduced by Hinkel in his publication series “The Archaeological Map of the Sudan“ the photographs were divided into small format pictures (KB) in black and white (sw) and in color (co) and 6x6 sized pictures (MF/ film abbreviation R) in black and white (sw) and color (co). The slides were numbered according to Dr. Hinkel's own notes including slide numbers as well as film and picture numbers. The metadata for all photographs and slides is based on Hinkel's note in his photo and slide inventories and represent his own interpretations of the subject matter.

Unless stated otherwise, all pictures were made by Friedrich W. Hinkel. The copyright rests with the DAI.
